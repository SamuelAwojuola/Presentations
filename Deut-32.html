Deuteronomy 32

The rise, fall and restoration of Israel

“Yahweh will judge his people and have compassion on his servants when he sees their strength is gone…” [Deu 32:36]

Synopsis

Synopsis

The prophecy of Moses recorded in Deuteronomy 32 is all about the rise, fall and restoration of Israel. In this prophecy, Moses speaks of the fact that Israel will sin against God, abandoning him and serving idols.

He also, more importantly, declares what God will do in response to their unfaithfulness, having been provoked to jealousy and anger and what God would do and not do for the sake of His Name.

“...I know how rebellious and stiffnecked you are. If you have been rebellious against the Lord while I am still alive and with you, how much more will you rebel after I die! 28Assemble before me all the elders of your tribes and all your officials, so that I can speak these words in their hearing and call heaven and earth to testify against them.
29For I know that after my death you are sure to become utterly corrupt and to turn from the way I have commanded you. In days to come, disaster will fall upon you because you will do evil in the sight of the Lord and provoke him to anger by what your hands have made.” 30And Moses recited the words of this song from beginning to end in the hearing of the whole assembly of Israel:” [Deu 31:27-30]

What we here try to do is breakdown the prophecy, laying out its main characters, conflicts and events (among other things).

Main Characters in Deuteronomy 32 Prophecy

Main Characters in Deuteronomy 32 Prophecy
  1. Yahweh
  2. Yahweh’s people (i.e., Israel, also called Jeshurun in this prophecy [Deu 32:15 cf., Deu 33:5,26])
  3. The sons of the Most High
  4. The Wicked, i.e. the antichrist
  5. The rock of the enemies, i.e. the devil
  6. The nations

Major Events & Turning Points in Deuteronomy 32 Eschatology

Major Events & Turning Points in Deuteronomy 32 Eschatology
  1. Jeshurun is saved and made a nation by Yahweh
  2. Jeshurun betrays Yahweh
  3. Yahweh gives up Jeshurun to trouble at the hand of other nations
  4. Yahweh avenges his people
  5. Yahweh saves his people in mercy and so as not to allow the enemy to make a false claim that his people were not destroyed by Yahweh but by them

The Events of Deuteronomy 32

The Events of Deuteronomy 32
  1. The nations are given their inheritance [Deu 32:7-8 cf., Gen 11:1-9; Act 17:26ff]
  2. The nations are put under the sons of God—gods [Deu 32:8 cf., Psa 82; Dan 10:13,20]
  • Most translations will have “according to the number of the sons of Israel”. However, there is an alternative translation, depending on the manuscripts, for example, the Septuagint, that reads “according to the sons of God”.
    • The Most High assigned nations their lands; he determined where peoples should live. He assigned to each nation a heavenly being, but Jacob's descendants he chose for himself. [Good News Translation]
    • When the Most High assigned lands to the nations, when he divided up the human race, he established the boundaries of the peoples according to the number in his heavenly court. “For the people of Israel belong to the Lord; Jacob is his special possession. [New Living Translation]
    • When God divided up the world among the nations, He gave each of them a supervising angel! But he appointed none for Israel; For Israel was God's own personal possession! [The Living Bible]
  • You may find the alternative translation in the footnotes of translations that read “sons of Israel”.
  • There is no contradiction here, for the nations were divided into 70 [Gen 10 you will have to count], and the number of the children of Jacob (also called Israel) who went into Egypt was 70 [Gen 46:27; Exo 1:5].
  • So, when God divided men into nations at the Tower of Babel, recorded in [Gen 11:1-9], God placed them under angelic beings, also called “sons of God” [Psa 82:6 (but for whatever bias one already holds, these are clearly not men, as it makes no sense to threaten men that they will die like men); Job 1:6; 2:1 (some translations will have ‘angels’, but what is originally there is “sons of God”)].
  • This is why there are such things as we read in Daniel, “the prince of Persia” [Dan 10:13] and “the prince of Greece” [Dan 10:20], and even Michael, “the prince of Israel” [Dan 10:13,21; 12:1]. These are all angels, and none is a man.
  1. Yahweh’s portion is his people Israel and the land of Canaan is their inheritance [Deu 32:9]
  2. Yahweh saves his people and leads them from the wilderness to a good land. His people are well fed and taken care of [Deu 32:10-14]
  1. Jeshurun (his people), after growing strong, rejects Yahweh and goes after foreign gods to whom he owes nothing having no share in his creation nor in his birth [Deu 32:15-18]
  2. This makes Yahweh very jealous and he becomes very angry with his people [Deu 32:19]
  1. He decides to repay them in kind,
    • He will punish them for their sins, hiding his face from them and thus rejecting them just as they rejected him [Deu 32:20,22] and
    • As they have made him envious by choosing gods who were really no god, he too will make them envious by choosing a nation that was really no nation [Deu 32:21] this nation is a senseless nation [cf. Deu 32:28-29].
  1. Yahweh will turn on his own people almost to the point of destroying them completely, exterminating them from memory. He will, however, refrain from doing so at the thought of his enemy claiming victory [Deu 32:23-26] and taking the credit for the fate of His people [Deu 32:27]
  • The senselessness of these nations is expressed in the facts that
    • These same nations are the same nations by whom Yahweh will make his people jealous as they are senseless [Deu 32:28-29]
    • They have no discernment of their end [Deu 32:29]
    • Though they themselves know that Yahweh the Rock of Israel is stronger than their own rock, [Deu 32:31] they do not realize that they could not have had victory over Yahweh’s people if he had not given them up [Deu 32:30]
    • They do not have righteous laws like those of Yahweh gave his people [Deu 32:31-33] Their rock is unlike Yahweh in power and in wisdom
  1. There is a day appointed for the destruction of Yahweh’s enemies [Deu 32:40-42]
  2. Yahweh will destroy the enemy nations in vengeance and repayment for the treatment of his people [Deu 32:35]
  3. Yahweh will judge his people [Deu 32:36]
  4. Yahweh will have compassion on his servants at the end [Deu 32:36]
